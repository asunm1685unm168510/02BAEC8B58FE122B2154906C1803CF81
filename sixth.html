Delicious Sambar Recipe

Enjoy this traditional South Indian dish with rice or dosa!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 medium-sized onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 potato, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons Sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil (for tempering)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the toor dal in water and cook it in a pressure cooker with 2 cups of water for about 3-4 whistles until soft and mushy.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add dried red chilies and curry leaves.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Now, add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  5. Add diced carrots and potatoes, and sauté for 2-3 minutes. Then, add the tamarind pulp, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Stir well.
  6. Pour in 4 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Let the vegetables cook until tender.
  7. Add the cooked toor dal to the pot and mix everything well. Let it simmer for 10 minutes to blend the flavors together.
  8. Once done, garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice, dosa, or idli.

Note:

You can adjust the spiciness by adding more or less sambar powder according to your preference. You can also add other vegetables like pumpkin or brinjal for variation.
